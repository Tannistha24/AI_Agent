Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team. His journey from a promising young talent to a global cricketing icon is marked by exceptional dedication, consistent performance, and numerous record-breaking achievements across all formats of the game. Kohli's early career saw him hone his skills in domestic cricket, quickly rising through the ranks to make his international debut, where he soon established himself as one of the world's premier batsmen.

Throughout his illustrious career, Kohli has amassed an astonishing array of records. In One Day Internationals (ODIs), he holds the record for the most centuries, surpassing legendary figures. His prowess in Test cricket is equally impressive, with 9,230 runs in 123 matches, including 30 centuries and seven double hundreds. He is renowned for his ability to perform under pressure and excel in challenging conditions, both at home and abroad. In the T20 International format, Kohli has achieved the highest career average, won the most Man of the Series awards (seven), and was the first Indian to score 10,000 T20 runs, a testament to his versatility and dominance.

Beyond his batting exploits, Virat Kohli's captaincy significantly impacted Indian cricket. He holds several captaincy records, including the most Test victories as an Indian captain, nine consecutive Test victories, and nine consecutive ODI victories. Under his leadership, India achieved considerable success, including winning the Test Mace five times, solidifying their position as a top-ranked team. His aggressive yet inspirational leadership style fostered a winning mentality within the squad.

Kohli's exceptional performances have earned him widespread recognition and numerous accolades. He has received prestigious ICC awards, national honors, and individual distinctions such as the Player of the Tournament in the ODI World Cup 2023. These awards underscore his status as a decorated cricketer and an influential figure in the sport. His passion, intensity, and unwavering commitment to fitness have set new benchmarks for modern cricketers.

Recently, Kohli has made significant career decisions, announcing his retirement from T20 Internationals after India's T20 World Cup triumph in 2024, and from Test cricket in May 2025, to focus primarily on One-Day Internationals. These choices reflect his evolving priorities and desire to prolong his career in the format where he has consistently excelled. Despite these transitions, Virat Kohli's legacy as a cricket legend is firmly cemented. His inspiring journey, phenomenal achievements, and profound impact on the game will continue to inspire generations of cricketers and fans worldwide, ensuring his place among the all-time greats of cricket.
